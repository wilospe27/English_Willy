1Present Simple

Cómo reconocerlo

Estructura: Sujeto + verbo (+s/es si es he/she/it) · negativo: don't/doesn't + verbo · pregunta: Do/Does + sujeto + verbo?
SingularPlural
1ra personaIwe
2da personayouyou
3ra personahe / she / itthey

Palabras señal:

alwaysusuallyoften sometimesneverevery day/week on Mondaysgenerally
Nemotecnia: piensa en una serpiente — "He/She/It hace SSSS" (agrega -s). Es el tiempo de las rutinas: si lo haces "siempre, nunca, a veces", es Present Simple.
Regla de oro 👉 Las terminaciones especiales SOLO se agregan en oraciones afirmativas con He / She / It (3ra persona singular). Con I, you, we, they el verbo se queda en su forma base.
  • Regla general → agrega -s: she calls Juan · he drinks water
  • Verbos terminados en o, x, z, s, ss, sh, ch → agrega -es: go → goes · watch → watches
  • Verbos terminados en consonante + y → cambia a -ies: try → tries · study → studies (John studies English)
  • have es irregular → se convierte en has: Carmen has a car (pero "They have a house", porque they no es 3ra persona singular)

20 ejercicios — completa con la forma correcta

  1. She (work) in the finance department.
  2. They (send) invoices every Friday.
  3. He (not / attend) the Monday meetings.
  4. you (check) your email every morning?
  5. The manager (review) reports weekly.
  6. We (not / have) enough budget this quarter.
  7. I usually (arrive) at the office at 8 AM.
  8. she (approve) purchase orders?
  9. The team (meet) every Tuesday.
  10. He (not / like) long meetings.
  11. My colleagues (prepare) the agenda in advance.
  12. It (take) two days to process an invoice.
  13. they (negotiate) contracts often?
  14. The client (pay) invoices on time.
  15. We (follow) up with clients every week.
  16. She (not / work) on Fridays.
  17. The CEO (give) a speech every year.
  18. you (need) more information?
  19. He (report) directly to the director.
  20. The company (not / export) to that region.

2Present Continuous

Cómo reconocerlo

Estructura: Sujeto + am/is/are + verbo-ING · negativo: am/is/are + not + V-ing · pregunta: Am/Is/Are + sujeto + V-ing?
nowright nowat the moment currentlythese daysLook!Listen!
Nemotecnia: "-ING = está pasando AHORA, como una película en PLAY". Si puedes decir "mira, en este momento..." antes de la frase, es Present Continuous.
Regla de oro 👉 El verbo be tiene que coincidir siempre con el sujeto — nunca existe un Present Continuous sin am/is/are:
  • I → am
  • He / She / It → is
  • You / We / They → are
El verbo principal SIEMPRE termina en -ing. Sin am/is/are, no es Present Continuous.

20 ejercicios — completa con la forma correcta

  1. She (work) on the budget right now.
  2. I (write) the report at the moment.
  3. They (negotiate) the contract this week.
  4. he (attend) the meeting now?
  5. We (not / use) that system currently.
  6. The team (prepare) the agenda for tomorrow.
  7. you (review) the invoice?
  8. Management (discuss) the new policy right now.
  9. I (not / wait) for approval anymore.
  10. She (call) the client at this moment.
  11. They (update) the database this afternoon.
  12. we (lose) money this quarter?
  13. He (analyze) the report currently.
  14. The clients (wait) for a response.
  15. I (look) for the invoice number now.
  16. she (present) the proposal today?
  17. We (not / meet) this week because of the holiday.
  18. The system (process) the payment right now.
  19. They (plan) next year's budget currently.
  20. you (follow) up with the supplier?

3Past Simple

Cómo reconocerlo

Estructura: Sujeto + verbo+ED (regular) o forma irregular · negativo: did not (didn't) + verbo base · pregunta: Did + sujeto + verbo base?
yesterdaylast week/month/year... ago in 2020when I was...
Nemotecnia: "-ED = ENDED" (terminó). La acción ya cerró, sin conexión con el presente. Ojo con los irregulares: go→went, see→saw, write→wrote (memorízalos en grupos de 5).
Regla de oro 👉 El pasado simple es IGUAL para todos los sujetos — nunca se agrega -s, ni siquiera con he/she/it:
  • Verbos regulares: raíz + -ed (work → worked)
  • Verbos irregulares: forma propia que se memoriza aparte (go → went, not "goed")
  • En negativos y preguntas, el verbo vuelve a su forma base: did not go, nunca "did not went"

20 ejercicios — completa con la forma correcta

  1. She (send) the invoice yesterday.
  2. They (approve) the budget last month.
  3. He (not / attend) the meeting last week.
  4. you (finish) the report?
  5. We (negotiate) the contract two years ago.
  6. I (not / receive) the email last Friday.
  7. The manager (review) the proposal yesterday.
  8. she (call) the client this morning?
  9. They (sign) the agreement last year.
  10. He (go) to the conference last week.
  11. We (not / have) enough stock last quarter.
  12. I (write) the report last night.
  13. they (pay) the invoice on time?
  14. The company (launch) the product in 2023.
  15. She (leave) the office early yesterday.
  16. We (meet) the new supplier last Tuesday.
  17. he (send) the follow-up email?
  18. They (not / approve) the request last time.
  19. I (see) the report two days ago.
  20. The director (give) the presentation last Friday.

4Present Perfect

Cómo reconocerlo

Estructura: Sujeto + have/has + participio pasado (V3) · negativo: have/has + not + V3 · pregunta: Have/Has + sujeto + V3?
alreadyjustyet everneversince forso far
Nemotecnia: "HAVE = tengo la experiencia" — conecta el pasado con el presente, sin decir cuándo exactamente. Truco: "since" = desde un punto (since 2019), "for" = durante un periodo (for 3 years).
Regla de oro 👉 Necesitas SIEMPRE dos piezas juntas: el auxiliar correcto + el participio pasado (V3) — nunca el verbo en presente ni en pasado simple:
  • He / She / It → has
  • I / You / We / They → have
  • El verbo principal siempre es participio pasado: gone, worked, written — nunca "go" ni "went"

20 ejercicios — completa con la forma correcta

  1. She (already / send) the invoice.
  2. We (not / receive) the payment yet.
  3. you (ever / work) in finance?
  4. They (just / finish) the report.
  5. He (work) here since 2019.
  6. I (not / see) the new policy yet.
  7. The team (complete) the project so far.
  8. she (approve) the budget yet?
  9. We (know) each other for five years.
  10. He (never / miss) a deadline.
  11. They (already / sign) the contract.
  12. I (not / finish) the presentation yet.
  13. you (read) the report?
  14. The company (grow) a lot since last year.
  15. We (have) three meetings so far this week.
  16. She (just / call) the client.
  17. they (ever / negotiate) with that supplier?
  18. He (not / submit) the report yet.
  19. I (work) on this proposal for two weeks.
  20. The manager (already / review) the numbers.

5Future — will / going to

Cómo reconocerlo

WILL + verbo base → predicciones, promesas, decisiones espontáneas.
Am/is/are + GOING TO + verbo base → planes ya decididos, intenciones.
tomorrownext weeksoon I think / probably (will)already arranged (going to)
Nemotecnia: "WILL = decisión del momento / promesa" (se decide al hablar). "GOING TO = ya lo decidí antes, voy en camino a hacerlo" (piensa en "GO" = ya vas hacia esa acción).
Regla de oro 👉 Will es para decisiones que tomas en el momento o predicciones; going to es para planes que ya decidiste ANTES de hablar:
  • will + verbo base → "I will call you" (lo decido ahora)
  • am/is/are + going to + verbo base → "We are going to launch" (ya estaba decidido)
  • Nunca se combinan los dos: "will going to" no existe

20 ejercicios — completa con la forma correcta

  1. I think we (win) the contract.
  2. We (launch) the new product next month (ya planeado).
  3. She (call) you back as soon as she can.
  4. They (meet) the client tomorrow at 10 AM (ya organizado).
  5. you (attend) the conference next week?
  6. I (not / finish) the report today, I'm sure.
  7. We (present) the results next Friday (ya acordado).
  8. He (probably / be) late.
  9. they (invest) in the project (plan)?
  10. I promise I (send) it tonight.
  11. The company (expand) to new markets next year (plan).
  12. It (rain) tomorrow, according to the forecast.
  13. We (not / change) the schedule (decisión ya tomada).
  14. she (join) the team next month (plan)?
  15. I (help) you with that, don't worry.
  16. They (sign) the contract next week (ya organizado).
  17. he (retire) next year (plan)?
  18. I think the price (increase).
  19. We (open) a new office in Bogotá (ya decidido).
  20. you (need) more staff?

6Modal verbs

Cómo reconocerlo

Estructura fija: Sujeto + modal + verbo base (sin "to", sin -s, sin -ing). No cambia con he/she/it.
can = habilidad/permisoshould = consejo must = obligacióncould = posibilidad/pedido cortés may/might = posibilidad
Nemotecnia: CAN = "CApacidad Now" (puedo ahora) · MUST = "Mandatory" (obligatorio, sin opción) · SHOULD = "Sugerencia" (consejo, no obligación) · MIGHT/MAY = "quizás, tal vez" (probabilidad baja).
Regla de oro 👉 Después de un modal (can, must, should, could, may, might) el verbo SIEMPRE va en forma base — sin "to", sin -s, sin -ing:
  • Correcto: She can speak.
  • Incorrecto: "She can to speak" / "She cans speak" / "She can speaking"
Los modales nunca cambian con he/she/it (no llevan -s).

20 ejercicios — completa con el modal correcto y el verbo

  1. You (must / submit) the report by Friday.
  2. She (can / speak) three languages.
  3. I (may / leave) early today?
  4. You (should / confirm) the invoice before paying.
  5. We (must not / share) confidential data.
  6. He (could / attend) the meeting if he finishes on time.
  7. you (can / send) me the file?
  8. They (might / approve) the budget next week.
  9. You (should not / miss) the deadline.
  10. she (must / work) overtime?
  11. We (can / negotiate) a better price.
  12. He (should / check) the numbers again.
  13. I (could / ask) you a question?
  14. They (must / follow) the new policy.
  15. It (might / rain), so bring an umbrella.
  16. You (can / call) me anytime.
  17. She (should / update) the report today.
  18. we (may / postpone) the meeting?
  19. He (must not / forget) the deadline.
  20. They (could / offer) a discount if we buy in bulk.

7Conditionals — 1st y 2nd

Cómo reconocerlo

1st (real/probable): If + present simple, ... will + verbo base.
2nd (hipotético/imaginario): If + past simple, ... would + verbo base.
ifif I were you (2nd)will (1st)would (2nd)
Nemotecnia: 1st Conditional = REAL y PROBABLE (presente + futuro: "si pasa, pasará"). 2nd Conditional = IMAGINARIO (pasado con significado presente: "si fuera, haría" — ojo: siempre "if I WERE", nunca "if I was" en contexto formal).
Regla de oro 👉 Después de "if" nunca uses "will"; y en el segundo condicional usa siempre "were" (no "was") para todos los sujetos:
  • 1st: If + presente simple, ... will + verbo base — "If it rains, we will cancel."
  • 2nd: If + pasado simple, ... would + verbo base — "If I were you, I would accept."
Nunca digas "if it will rain" — el "will" NUNCA va después de "if".

  4. If we (have) more budget, we (hire) more staff.
  5. If it (rain), the event (be) cancelled.
  6. If I (win) the lottery, I (invest) it.
  7. If you (send) the invoice today, we (pay) it tomorrow.
  8. If the company (grow), it (open) new offices.
  9. If he (not / study), he (fail) the exam.
  10. If I (have) the report now, I (send) it.
  11. If they (approve) the budget, we (start) the project.
  12. If she (be) the manager, she (change) the policy.
  13. If we (not / meet) the deadline, we (lose) the client.
  14. If I (know) the answer, I (tell) you.
  15. If you (call) me, I (answer).
  16. If the price (increase), demand (fall).
  17. If we (have) more time, we (do) a better analysis.
  18. If he (arrive) late, he (miss) the meeting.
  19. If I (be) in your position, I (negotiate) harder.

Cómo usar esta guía sin olvidar: resuelve un tiempo verbal por día (20 ejercicios), corrige con "Comprobar respuestas", y al día siguiente repite solo los que fallaste. Repite el ciclo completo cada 2 semanas — la repetición espaciada es lo que fija la regla en la memoria a largo plazo.
