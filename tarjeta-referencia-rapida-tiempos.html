Tarjeta de referencia rápida — los 12 tiempos verbales

Cruza el auxiliar (la estructura del verbo) con la palabra señal (el marcador de tiempo) para identificar cualquier tiempo en segundos.

Método: 1) mira el auxiliar → te dice la familia (simple, continuo, perfecto o perfecto continuo). 2) mira la palabra señal → te confirma si es presente, pasado o futuro. Cuando las dos coinciden, ya tienes el tiempo exacto.
Presente

Present Simple

verbo+s (he/she/it) · verbo base (yo/tú/we/they)
Señales: every day, usually, always, often, on Mondays
She reviews the report every day.

Present Continuous

am / is / are + verbo-ing
Señales: right now, at the moment, currently, today
He is preparing the report right now.

Present Perfect

have / has + participio (V3)
Señales: already, just, never, ever, yet, since, for
They have already signed the contract.

Present Perfect Continuous

have / has been + verbo-ing
Señales: for + duración, since + punto de inicio, lately
She has been working here for five years.
Pasado

Past Simple

verbo+ed · forma irregular fija
Señales: yesterday, last week, ... ago, in 2020
He called the client yesterday.

Past Continuous

was / were + verbo-ing
Señales: while, when + otra acción pasada, at that time
She was writing the report when he called.

Past Perfect

had + participio (V3)
Señales: before, by the time, already / just (en pasado)
They had finished before the meeting started.

Past Perfect Continuous

had been + verbo-ing
Señales: for + duración antes de otro pasado, before that
He had been working there for six months before the change.
Futuro

Future Simple

will + verbo base
Señales: tomorrow, next week/month/year, soon
We will call the client tomorrow.

Future Continuous

will be + verbo-ing
Señales: this time tomorrow, at [hora] tomorrow
He will be traveling this time tomorrow.

Future Perfect

will have + participio (V3)
Señales: by + fecha, by the time, by next month
They will have finished by Friday.

Future Perfect Continuous

will have been + verbo-ing
Señales: by + fecha + for + duración
She will have been working here for ten years by June.
